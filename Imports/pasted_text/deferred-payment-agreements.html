Remove the staleness warning note. --- ## PAGE 8: DEFERRED PAYMENT AGREEMENTS (Sub-page) **Breadcrumb:** Home > Adult social care > Paying for your care > Deferred payment agreements **H1:** Deferred payment agreements **Standfirst:** If you own your home and need to move permanently into a care home, a deferred payment agreement may help you pay for your care without having to sell your home straight away. ### What a deferred payment agreement is A deferred payment agreement is an arrangement between you and the council. The council pays part of your care home fees on your behalf, and in return places a legal charge against your property (similar to a mortgage). The amount the council pays builds up as a debt, which is repaid later — usually when the property is sold, or from your estate after your death. This means you do not have to sell your home during your lifetime to pay for care. You can also choose to repay the debt from another source at any time. ### Who is eligible Under the Care Act 2014, the council has a duty to offer a deferred payment agreement to everyone who meets the eligibility criteria. To be eligible, you must: - Have been assessed by Adult Social Care as needing to move permanently into a care home - Have non-property capital (savings, investments, and other assets excluding the value of your home) of less than £23,250 - Own or have part legal ownership of a property that would be included in your financial assessment - Have agreement from all parties with a legal interest in the property to enter the scheme - Ensure the property is registered with the Land Registry (if it is not, you must arrange this at your own expense) - Have no other beneficial interests on the property that would prevent a legal charge being placed on it (for example, an outstanding mortgage or equity release) ### The 12-week property disregard When you first move into permanent residential care, the value of your property is not counted in your financial assessment for the first 12 weeks. During this period, you will only need to pay a contribution based on your income and non-property capital. The deferred payment agreement starts after this 12-week period ends. ### How much you can defer The amount you can defer is based on the value of your property. In Waltham Forest, the council will defer up to 90% of the property value, minus an allowance of £14,250 (the lower capital limit). A professional valuation of your property will be needed. The council can arrange this, or you can arrange your own. ### What you pay from your income Even with a deferred payment agreement, you will still pay a contribution from your income each week towards the cost of your care. This is calculated through your financial assessment in the same way as for any other care home resident. Under a deferred payment agreement, you are allowed to keep a higher amount of your income each week to help with the upkeep of your property. The exact amount you can retain will be confirmed as part of your agreement. *Note: The amount you can retain under a deferred payment was £144 per week in 2015. This figure should be confirmed with the Financial Assessment team for the current year as it may have been updated.* ### Interest The council charges interest on the deferred amount. The interest rate is set by the government and is based on the gilt rate published in the Office for Budget Responsibility's Economic and Fiscal Outlook. The rate changes on 1 January and 1 July each year. Interest is charged from the date your deferred payment agreement starts and is compounded daily. The current interest rate will be confirmed when you apply. You will receive regular statements (at least every six months) showing how the charge is building up and what the total outstanding amount is. You can request a statement at any time. ### Set-up costs There are costs involved in setting up a deferred payment agreement. These may include: - Property valuation - Drawing up a legal agreement - Placing a legal charge on your property - Land Registry searches and other legal costs You can pay these costs upfront, or they can be added to the deferred amount. If they are added to the deferred amount, they will be subject to interest. ### Rental income If you decide to rent out your property while you are in the care home, the rental income will be included in your financial assessment. This will increase the contribution you pay from your income each week, which reduces the amount being deferred and the eventual debt. ### Top-up payments If your care home costs more than the council's agreed rate, the difference (the top-up) can sometimes be included within the deferred payment agreement. This means the top-up amount is added to the deferred debt rather than needing to be paid separately by a third party. Discuss this with the Financial Assessment team when you apply. ### How the agreement ends The deferred payment agreement ends when: - You sell your property and repay the debt - You choose to repay the debt from another source - You pass away, in which case the debt must be repaid from your estate within 90 days The council cannot cancel the agreement without your consent. ### What happens if your debt reaches the limit If the total deferred amount approaches the maximum (90% of the property value minus £14,250), the council will contact you to discuss your options. This might include selling the property, finding another way to pay, or reviewing your financial situation. ### Your responsibilities If you enter into a deferred payment agreement, you must: - Maintain the property in a reasonable condition - Keep the property insured (buildings insurance) - Inform the council of any changes that affect the property or your financial situation - Not take out any additional loans or charges against the property without the council's agreement ### Refusing or appealing a decision If the council refuses your application for a deferred payment, you will be told the reason in writing. You have the right to appeal the decision. Contact the Financial Assessment team to discuss your options. ### Get independent advice first **Callout box:** A deferred payment agreement is a significant financial and legal decision. You should get independent financial and legal advice before entering into one. The council can provide information about how the scheme works, but we cannot advise you on whether it is the right option for your personal circumstances. See our page on getting financial advice for information about independent advisers, including the Society of Later Life Advisers (SOLLA) which specialises in care funding advice. ### How to apply To apply for a deferred payment agreement, or to find out more, contact the Financial Assessment team. **Phone:** 020 8496 3130 **Email:** residentialfau@walthamforest.gov.uk *Note for implementation: Confirm that the Financial Assessment Unit email (residentialfau@walthamforest.gov.uk) and the phone numbers previously listed in the 2015 factsheet (020 8496 3404, 020 8496 3492, 020 8496 3490) are still correct.
