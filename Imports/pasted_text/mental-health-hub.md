## IMPORTANT: SCOPE AND POSITIONING

This section sits within the Adult Social Care part of the website. It covers mental health support for adults (aged 18 and over) only.

Content about mental health support for young people (under 18) and for families and parents is NOT included here. That content belongs in a different section of the council website (for example, under Families, young people and children, or under the broader Health and wellbeing area).

The section covers three things:
1. Where to get help right now (crisis and urgent support)
2. How to access talking therapies and professional support
3. How adult social care works alongside the NHS for people with ongoing mental health conditions

## PAGE 2: URGENT MENTAL HEALTH SUPPORT (Sub-page)

**Breadcrumb:** Home > Adult social care > Mental health and wellbeing > Urgent mental health support

**H1:** Urgent mental health support

**Standfirst:** If you or someone you know is in a mental health crisis, help is available now. You do not need to wait for a referral or an appointment.

### In an emergency

If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 999.

If you are at A&E, tell the staff that you are experiencing a mental health crisis. They will make sure you see someone who can help.

### Mental Health Direct

If you are in a crisis and do not know what to do, call Mental Health Direct on 0800 995 1000. This is a free, 24/7 helpline provided by NELFT (North East London NHS Foundation Trust) for people living in Waltham Forest. You can call at any time of day or night.

The team can talk you through what you are experiencing, help you decide what to do next, and connect you to the right service.

### Samaritans

If you need to talk to someone, call the Samaritans on 116 123. The call is free and the service is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. You do not have to be suicidal to call — you can talk about anything that is troubling you.

You can also email jo@samaritans.org if you prefer to write.

### Shout

If you prefer to text, text SHOUT to 85258. Shout is a free, confidential, 24/7 text messaging support service for anyone who is struggling to cope. You will be connected to a trained volunteer.

### NHS 111

If you need urgent medical advice but it is not an emergency, call 111. You can also go to 111.nhs.uk. NHS 111 can help you access mental health crisis services in your area.

### If you are worried about someone else

If you are concerned that someone you know is having a mental health crisis, you can call Mental Health Direct on 0800 995 1000 for advice on how to help them. If they are in immediate danger, call 999.

## PAGE 3: LOOKING AFTER YOUR MENTAL WELLBEING (Sub-page)

**Breadcrumb:** Home > Adult social care > Mental health and wellbeing > Looking after your mental wellbeing

**H1:** Looking after your mental wellbeing

**Standfirst:** There are practical things you can do every day to support your mental health. If you are going through a difficult time, these resources can help.

### Five ways to wellbeing

Research shows that five simple actions can make a real difference to how you feel:

- **Connect** — spend time with other people. Social connections help you feel supported and build resilience.
- **Be active** — any kind of physical activity can improve your mood. It does not need to be intense — a walk, some gardening, or gentle exercise all count.
- **Take notice** — pay attention to the world around you. Noticing your thoughts and feelings, and the things you see and hear, can help you feel more grounded.
- **Keep learning** — trying something new or building a skill can boost your confidence and give you a sense of purpose.
- **Give** — helping others, volunteering, or simply being kind to someone can improve your own wellbeing.

### Free online resources

These resources are available at any time and can help you manage stress, low mood, anxiety, or difficulty sleeping:

- **Good Thinking** — an NHS-approved digital wellbeing service covering anxiety, low mood, sleeping difficulties, and stress (good-thinking.uk)
- **Every Mind Matters** — includes a personalised mind plan based on your answers to a short quiz, with practical tips and advice (nhs.uk/every-mind-matters)
- **Thrive LDN** — a London-wide partnership with resources to support your mental wellbeing and ways to help others (thriveldn.co.uk)

### Social prescribing

If you feel isolated, disconnected, or unsure where to turn, a social prescribing link worker can help you find local activities, groups, and services that could support your wellbeing. This might include exercise groups, creative activities, volunteering, or social clubs.

Social prescribing is free and available through your GP. You can also contact the Waltham Forest Social Prescribing Service directly.

### Staying active

Physical activity is one of the most effective ways to support your mental health. The council offers a range of opportunities to get active in Waltham Forest, from parks and open spaces to leisure centres and community sports programmes.

## PAGE 4: TALKING THERAPIES AND PROFESSIONAL SUPPORT (Sub-page)

**Breadcrumb:** Home > Adult social care > Mental health and wellbeing > Talking therapies and professional support

**H1:** Talking therapies and professional support

**Standfirst:** If you are struggling with low mood, anxiety, stress, or other mental health difficulties, free professional support is available. You do not always need a GP referral.

### Waltham Forest Talking Therapies

Waltham Forest Talking Therapies is a free NHS service for adults living in the borough. It offers a range of therapies including cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT), counselling, and guided self-help for common mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, and stress.

You can refer yourself directly — you do not need a GP referral. Your GP can also refer you if you prefer.

**Button:** Refer yourself to Waltham Forest Talking Therapies
(Links to: talkingtherapies.nelft.nhs.uk/waltham-forest-)

### What to expect

After you refer yourself or your GP refers you, the service will contact you to arrange an initial assessment. This is a conversation about what you are experiencing and what kind of support might help.

You may be offered individual therapy, group therapy, or online support, depending on your needs. There may be a wait before your treatment starts, but the team will keep you informed.

### Adult Early Help Service

If you are going through a difficult life event — such as a bereavement, relationship breakdown, job loss, or health diagnosis — and it is affecting your daily life, the council's Adult Early Help Service may be able to help.

A link worker can support you to get back on track by helping you identify your goals and connecting you to local services. This is not a clinical mental health service, but it can make a real difference when you are going through a tough time.

### Through your GP

Your GP can discuss your mental health with you and may offer:

- A referral to talking therapies (or you can self-refer directly)
- Medication if appropriate
- A referral to specialist mental health services if your needs are more complex

If you are not registered with a GP, you can register using the NHS GP registration portal at nhs.uk.

### Private therapy

If you prefer, you can pay for private therapy. A one-hour session typically costs between £50 and £100. Make sure any therapist you choose is registered with an approved professional body, such as the BACP (British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy), the UKCP (UK Council for Psychotherapy), or the BPS (British Psychological Society).

### Other places that offer therapy

Some employers offer counselling through employee assistance programmes. Many colleges and universities provide free therapy to students. Local charities also offer low-cost or free therapy for specific issues, including Cruse for bereavement, Mind for mental health, and Relate for relationship difficulties.

## PAGE 5: ADULT SOCIAL CARE AND MENTAL HEALTH (Sub-page)

**Breadcrumb:** Home > Adult social care > Mental health and wellbeing > Adult social care and mental health

**H1:** Adult social care and mental health

**Standfirst:** If you have an ongoing mental health condition that affects your daily life, you may be eligible for support from adult social care. The council works alongside NHS mental health services to make sure you get the right help.

### How it works

In Waltham Forest, adult social care for people with mental health conditions is provided through a partnership between the council and NELFT (North East London NHS Foundation Trust). NELFT provides the NHS mental health services, and the council provides the social care support. The two work together so you do not have to navigate separate systems.

If you are already receiving support from an NHS mental health team, your care coordinator or key worker can help you access social care support. If you are not currently receiving NHS mental health services but think you might need social care, contact the Adult Social Care team.

### What support is available

If you are eligible for adult social care because of a mental health condition, you may be able to get help with:

- Managing daily tasks such as cooking, cleaning, and looking after yourself
- Staying safe at home
- Maintaining relationships and staying connected to your community
- Getting into or staying in work, education, or volunteering
- Finding suitable housing or supported accommodation
- Managing your finances and benefits

The kind of support you receive depends on your needs. It might include regular support at home, a personal budget to arrange your own care, or access to day services and community activities.

### NHS mental health teams in Waltham Forest

NELFT runs several specialist mental health teams in Waltham Forest that work closely with the council's adult social care service:

- **Mental Health and Wellness Teams** (Central, North, and South) — community teams supporting adults with a range of mental health conditions
- **Older Adults Mental Health Team** — specialist support for older people with mental health conditions including dementia
- **Early Intervention in Psychosis (EIP)** — early support for people experiencing psychosis for the first time
- **Community Recovery Team** — support for people with more complex or longer-term mental health conditions

If you are referred to one of these teams, they will work with the council to assess your health and social care needs together.

### Getting a mental health social care assessment

You can ask for a care needs assessment at any time by contacting the Adult Social Care team. If you are already working with an NHS mental health team, your care coordinator can also request one on your behalf.

The assessment looks at how your mental health condition affects your daily life and what support might help. It follows the same process as any adult social care assessment.

Phone: 020 8496 3000
Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm

### The Care Programme Approach (CPA)

If you have more complex mental health needs, your care may be coordinated under the Care Programme Approach. This means you will have a care coordinator who brings together your health and social care support into one plan. Your care plan is reviewed regularly to make sure it is still meeting your needs.

### Carers

If you care for someone with a mental health condition, you have the right to a carer's assessment. This looks at how caring affects your own life and what support might help you. See our carers section for more information.

**Callout box:** Mental health is not separate from adult social care. If your mental health condition affects your ability to manage daily life, you may be eligible for exactly the same kind of support — personal budgets, care plans, community services — as someone with a physical disability or long-term illness.

## PAGE 6: LOCAL MENTAL HEALTH SUPPORT SERVICES (Sub-page)

**Breadcrumb:** Home > Adult social care > Mental health and wellbeing > Local mental health support services

**H1:** Local mental health support services

**Standfirst:** There are many organisations in Waltham Forest that offer mental health support, peer groups, and specialist services. You do not need a referral to access most of them.

### Community support and peer groups

- **CREST WF** — social, befriending, educational and healthy living support for older people with a disability and people experiencing dementia (crestwf.org.uk)
- **Waltham Forest Hearing Voices Group** — a peer support group for people who hear voices
- **WF Bipolar UK Support Group** — peer support for people affected by bipolar disorder
- **Coping Through Football** — recovery-based football sessions for people with mental health conditions

### Support for specific communities

- **Black People's Mental Health Association (BPMHA)** — support for black and minority ethnic communities
- **LGBTQIA+ support** — local and national mental health services that understand the specific needs of LGBTQIA+ people. Services include MindOut (mindout.org.uk) and Switchboard (switchboard.lgbt)
- **SignHealth** — mental health support for deaf people
- **Empowering Deaf Society (EDS)** — a deaf-led charity supporting deaf people across London
- **Royal British Legion** — support for armed forces veterans and their families

### Bereavement support

- **Cruse Bereavement Support** — free helpline on 0808 808 1677 (Monday to Friday, 9:30am to 5pm) and local support groups (cruse.org.uk)
- **Grief in Pieces** — support for people who have lost someone to suicide
- **The Good Grief Trust** — resources and practical support for people experiencing bereavement

### Substance use and mental health

- **Change Grow Live (CGL)** — drug and alcohol support for Waltham Forest residents, including help for people whose substance use is connected to mental health difficulties

### Wider support

- **Body and Soul** — support for people affected by childhood trauma and adversity
- **Sister Circle** — support for women in difficult circumstances
- **Two Generations** — a homeshare programme connecting older people looking for companionship with younger people looking for affordable housing

### Find more services

The council maintains an online Wellbeing Directory with a full list of support and community services in the borough, searchable by category and location.
