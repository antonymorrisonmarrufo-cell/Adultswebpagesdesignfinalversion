## PAGE 2: HOW TO MAKE A REFERRAL (Sub-page)

**Breadcrumb:** Home > Adult social care > Information for care professionals > How to make a referral

**H1:** How to make a referral

**Standfirst:** If you are a GP, nurse, hospital discharge coordinator, social worker, or other professional and you need to refer someone for adult social care support in Waltham Forest, this page explains how.

### Current referral process

To refer someone for adult social care support, please complete the referral form and send it to the Adults Hub.

**Download:** Request for Help, Support, Protection or Safeguarding referral form (Word document)

**Email the completed form to:** WFDLiaison@walthamforest.gov.uk

**Phone (for urgent referrals or queries):** 020 8496 3130
Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm

*Note for implementation: Confirm the referral form filename and download link. The current form is titled "Request for Help, Support, Protection or Safeguarding V2.0" — confirm whether a newer version exists.*

### What to include in your referral

To help the Adults Hub process your referral quickly, please include:

- The person's full name, date of birth, and address
- Your name, role, organisation, and contact details
- The reason for referral and what has prompted it now
- A description of the person's current needs and situation
- Any known risks or safeguarding concerns
- Whether the person is aware of and consents to the referral
- Whether the person has mental capacity to make decisions about their care
- Details of any other professionals or services currently involved
- Any relevant medical or social history

Incomplete referrals may be returned, which delays the process.

### What happens after you refer

Once your referral is received:

1. The Adults Hub will check whether the person is already known to Adult Social Care.
2. The referral will be logged and allocated to the appropriate team based on the nature of the need (for example, Adults Care Management, Home First, Occupational Therapy, or Mental Health).
3. For safeguarding concerns, the referral will be screened by the Multi-Agency Safeguarding Hub (MASH).

You will not currently receive automatic confirmation that your referral has been received. If you need to check, phone the Adults Hub on 020 8496 3130.

### Safeguarding referrals

If your referral involves a safeguarding concern about an adult at risk, use the dedicated safeguarding process. See our safeguarding adults page for the online referral form and procedures.

### Mental health referrals

If the person's needs are primarily mental health related, the referral route is through NELFT, not the council's Adults Hub. Contact the NELFT Mental Health Single Point of Access on 0300 300 1570 (option 4) for adults aged 18–64, or the NELFT Older Adult Mental Health Team on 0300 555 1270 for adults aged 65+ or those with complex cognitive conditions.

### Hospital discharge referrals

If you are referring from Whipps Cross Hospital or the Ainslie Rehabilitation Unit, please send referrals directly to the Hospital Social Work Team, not to the general Adults Hub inbox. Contact your hospital social work liaison for the correct pathway.

### Occupational therapy referrals

For OT referrals, download and complete the Adult Social Care referral form (the same form used for other referrals) and email it to WFDLiaison@walthamforest.gov.uk. Alternatively, phone 020 8496 3130.

**Callout box:** We know the current referral process relies on Word documents and email. We are working to replace this with a structured online referral form that provides confirmation of receipt and the ability to track the progress of your referral. This is part of the Adults Digital Front Door programme.

## PAGE 3: SAFEGUARDING ADULTS (Sub-page)

**Breadcrumb:** Home > Adult social care > Information for care professionals > Safeguarding adults

**H1:** Safeguarding adults

**Standfirst:** How to raise a safeguarding concern about an adult at risk in Waltham Forest, and the multi-agency procedures that guide professional practice.

### When to raise a concern

You should raise a safeguarding concern if you believe an adult with care and support needs is experiencing, or is at risk of, abuse or neglect, and is unable to protect themselves because of those needs. This applies whether or not the person is receiving council-funded services.

If a crime is being or has been committed, or someone is in immediate danger, call the police on 999.

### How to raise a concern

**Online:** Complete the adult safeguarding referral form
(Links to the existing online safeguarding referral form)

**Phone:** 020 8496 3130

**Email:** MASHrequests@walthamforest.gov.uk

The Multi-Agency Safeguarding Hub (MASH) screens all safeguarding referrals. Your referral will be reviewed by a team that includes professionals from the council, health services, and the police.

### London Multi-Agency Adult Safeguarding Policy and Procedures

All professionals in Waltham Forest should follow the London multi-agency adult safeguarding policy and procedures. These set out how to identify, respond to, and investigate safeguarding concerns.

**Link:** London Multi-Agency Adult Safeguarding Policy and Procedures (external link)

### Adults threshold guidance

The Adults Threshold Guidance sets out the role and responsibilities of all agencies working together to ensure vulnerable adults are safeguarded in Waltham Forest. It helps practitioners determine the appropriate level of response to a concern.

**Download:** Adults Threshold Guidance (PDF)

### Managing allegations

If you need to manage an allegation against an adult who works with adults with care and support needs, refer to the procedures for managing allegations.

### Multi-agency escalation and professional challenge

If you believe your professional concerns have not been acted on appropriately, or if you disagree with a decision made by another agency, the Multi-agency escalation and professional challenge guidance sets out how to resolve professional disagreements through a structured four-step process.

## PAGE 5: COMMISSIONING AND PROVIDER INFORMATION (Sub-page)

**Breadcrumb:** Home > Adult social care > Information for care professionals > Commissioning and provider information

**H1:** Commissioning and provider information

**Standfirst:** Information for care providers about commissioning arrangements, market opportunities, and how we work with the provider market in Waltham Forest.

### Market Position Statements

The council publishes Market Position Statements to give care providers a clear picture of the local adult social care market. These cover demand, supply, quality, spend, and commissioning intentions across different service areas, including domiciliary care, residential care, supported living, and learning disability services.

**Link:** View Market Position Statements

### Fair Cost of Care

In 2022, the government required all councils to carry out a fair cost of care exercise. This established a shared understanding with providers of the local cost of delivering care, to ensure that the prices paid are sustainable and fair.

**Download:** Cost of Care Report — Residential and Nursing Care 65+ (PDF)
**Download:** Cost of Care Report — Domiciliary Care 18+ (PDF)

### Market Sustainability Plan

The Market Sustainability Plan sets out how the council intends to move towards paying a fair cost of care. It covers the current sustainability of local care markets, the impact of future changes, and the council's approach to addressing any issues.

**Download:** Market Sustainability Plan (PDF)

### Current frameworks and contracts

The council commissions adult social care services through a combination of framework agreements and spot-purchased contracts. Key frameworks include:

- Domiciliary care
- Supported living (split into four needs-based lots)
- Residential and nursing care
- Learning disability services

If you are a provider interested in working with Waltham Forest, or if you have questions about current or upcoming commissioning opportunities, contact the Adult Social Care Commissioning team.

### Provider support

The council has funded Care Provider's Voice, which offers a job broker scheme for care providers, opportunities to share training, and support for provider engagement within and outside the borough.
